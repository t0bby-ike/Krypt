Frequently Asked Questions

Find Answers to Common Questions About Krypts

General Questions

What is Krypts?
Krypts is a free online tool that allows you to encrypt and decrypt text using 256-bit AES encryption. It is designed to help you protect sensitive information and share it securely.
Is Krypts free to use?
Yes, Krypts is completely free to use. There are no hidden fees or subscriptions.
Do I need to create an account to use Krypts?
No, Krypts does not require you to create an account. You can start encrypting and decrypting text immediately.

Encryption and Decryption

How does Krypts encrypt text?
Krypts uses 256-bit AES encryption, one of the most secure encryption standards available. Your text is encrypted locally in your browser and never sent to our servers.
Can I decrypt text without the password?
No, the password is required to decrypt text. Without the correct password, the encrypted text cannot be decrypted.
Is my data stored on your servers?
No, Krypts does not store your data. All encryption and decryption processes occur locally in your browser.

Security and Privacy

Is Krypts secure?
Yes, Krypts uses industry-standard 256-bit AES encryption to ensure your data is secure. Additionally, your data is never stored or transmitted to our servers.
Can I trust Krypts with my sensitive information?
Absolutely. Krypts is designed with privacy and security in mind. Your data is encrypted locally, and we do not have access to it.
What happens if I forget my password?
Unfortunately, if you forget your password, there is no way to recover the encrypted text. We do not store passwords or encrypted data.

Technical Questions

What encryption algorithm does Krypts use?
Krypts uses the 256-bit AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) algorithm in CCM mode, which is widely recognized for its security and efficiency.
Can I use Krypts on mobile devices?
Yes, Krypts is fully responsive and works on all devices, including smartphones and tablets.
Does Krypts support file encryption?
Currently, Krypts only supports text encryption and decryption. File encryption is not available at this time.

Contact and Support

How can I contact Krypts support?
If you have any questions or need support, please visit our Contact Us page.
Where can I find more information about Krypts?
For more information, visit our About page or read our Privacy Policy.
